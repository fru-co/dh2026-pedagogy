Instructor-run AI experiment

AI-Generated Final Paper: Creation Conditions

This memo records how the Codex-generated final paper was produced. The paper is an instructor-run experiment, not a student submission.

Purpose

The experiment tested whether an AI system could produce a plausible student-style final paper from the course materials and locally available course data. The central requirement was not polished prose alone, but a comparative literary question, three categories of digital analysis, a return to close reading, a workflow log, and an honest account of limitations.

AI Environment

  • System: Codex
  • Recorded environment label: Codex 5.5 extra high
  • Date: July 10, 2026

The environment label is retained as experiment metadata so that this paper can be distinguished from outputs produced by other AI systems or settings.

Assignment and Course Materials

The AI consulted the following public-facing course materials:

  • Course syllabus
  • Final Paper Assignment
  • Week 10 handout
  • Week 11 handout
  • Vector Models for Literary Research
  • Transformers, BERT, SBERT, and Historical BERT

The paper compares Harriet Beecher Stowe’s “The Minister’s Housekeeper” and James McCune Smith’s “The Washerwoman.” These texts were selected because the course materials frame them around domestic labor, religion, gender, freedom, and semantic search.

Analyses Actually Run

The paper combines executed analysis with explicitly identified approximations.

Category 1: Frequency and KWIC

A small Python analysis counted and inspected selected word groups in the two course texts, including work*, labor/labour, house*, minister/parson, freedom/free, sing*, dunk, sabbath, hand*, talk*, and woman/women.

Counts were normalized per 1,000 word tokens because the two texts differ substantially in length.

Category 2: Word Vectors

The full pretrained GloVe workflow was not available in the experiment environment. The paper therefore uses a limited local approximation:

  • the course corpus only;
  • a five-word co-occurrence window;
  • PPMI weighting;
  • truncated SVD;
  • target words including minister, work, dunk, and freedom.

The paper identifies this as a weak substitute rather than presenting it as equivalent to a pretrained GloVe model.

Category 3: SBERT

The experiment used precomputed sentence embeddings from SBERT Text Lab with the all-MiniLM-L6-v2 model. It compared already embedded course-text sentences against one another rather than encoding a new natural-language query.

This is narrower than the normal SBERT Text Lab workflow, and the limitation is stated in the paper.

What Remains AI-Generated

Although some computations were executed, the comparative framing, close reading, interpretation, selection of evidence, and prose were generated by the AI. The numerical and textual outputs therefore do not make the paper equivalent to a human student’s documented reading process.

Known Limitations

  • The close readings and quotations require human verification against the primary texts.
  • The local word-vector experiment is too small to function as a robust embedding model.
  • The SBERT comparison uses existing sentence vectors rather than newly encoded queries.
  • No external web research was added to the course package.
  • No screenshots were produced during the experiment.

The paper is published to document a hybrid AI workflow and should be read as experimental evidence about that workflow, not as a model student submission.
