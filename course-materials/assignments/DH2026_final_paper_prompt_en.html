Course assignment

DH2026 Final Paper Assignment

1. Purpose

The goal of this paper is to formulate a question about the literary texts, test several Digital Humanities methods, and bring the results back to close reading. High technical sophistication is not required.

Tool outputs are not conclusions by themselves. Explain what the tools reveal, what they miss, and how the results confirm or complicate your reading. An experiment that did not work can still become meaningful analysis if you discuss it carefully.

2. Requirements

  • Deadline: July 20, 2026, 23:59
  • Submission: Google Classroom
  • Length: about 3,000–4,000 Japanese characters or 1,800–2,500 English words
  • Figures, screenshots, appendices, workflow logs, and AI-use notes do not count toward the length
  • Compare at least two literary texts from the course
  • Use at least one tool or method from each of the three categories below
  • Include a workflow log and a short note on AI use

3. Research Question and Texts

Compare the texts through a shared theme, word, image, character type, narrative form, genre, or historical problem. Possible topics include sin, morality, religion, labor, capitalism, domesticity, gender, the city, crowds, the body, madness, writing, reading, observation, movement, speed, and apocalypse.

A strong question should connect the comparison of the texts with the strengths or limitations of the digital methods. It is useful to ask where tool results confirm your close reading and where they diverge from it.

4. Required Method Categories

Category 1: Frequency, N-gram, KWIC, and Distribution

Possible tools include DH Reading Lab, Voyant Tools, Google Ngram Viewer, COHA, Google Books, and CLiC Fiction.

Identify the word, phrase, or cluster you examined; specify the texts, corpus, period, and other conditions; summarize the important result; and return to a specific passage.

Category 2: Word Vectors and Word Embeddings

Possible tools include Word Vector Explorer, HistWords Explorer, Word2Vec or GloVe-style models, and pretrained embeddings used through Google Colab.

Identify the target word, model, corpus, period, and settings. Discuss which neighboring words or semantic changes mattered, whether they matched your initial reading, and what new question emerged.

Category 3: Transformer-Based Models

Possible tools include SBERT Text Lab, Historical BERT Explorer, and other BERT, SBERT, or Transformer-based models.

Identify the query or example, model, text filter, period, unit, and settings. Compare the result with keyword search and explain how it led you back to the literary text.

5. Suggested Paper Structure

  1. Title and research question
  2. Texts and axis of comparison
  3. Initial close reading
  4. Analysis using the three method categories
  5. Comparison of the methods and return to specific passages
  6. Limits, unsuccessful experiments, unresolved questions, and possible next steps
  7. Workflow log
  8. Note on AI use

You may adjust this structure to fit your argument, but the paper should include close reading, all three method categories, comparison, and reflection.

6. Workflow Log

The workflow log is a concise record of what you actually tried. A table, bullet list, or screenshots with short explanations are all acceptable. Record:

  • tool or method
  • text or texts examined
  • search word, query, target word, phrase, or sentence
  • comparison conditions, including corpus, period, model, filter, or settings
  • main result
  • saved evidence, such as a screenshot, table, or note
  • passage reread after seeing the result

7. Use of AI

Generative AI is permitted, but outsourcing the assignment without reading the texts or running the tools yourself is inconsistent with the purpose of the course.

At the end of the paper, briefly state whether and how you used AI—for example, for language checking, brainstorming, revising a draft, or considering possible interpretations of tool results. The final interpretation must be grounded in your own reading and documented experiments.

8. Evaluation and Expectations

The paper will be evaluated on whether it:

  1. demonstrates careful reading of the literary texts
  2. compares at least two texts through a clear question
  3. uses all three method categories with clearly stated conditions
  4. connects tool results to specific passages
  5. reflects honestly on limitations, failures, and uncertainty
  6. includes a workflow log that makes the process visible
  7. explains AI use transparently

Finished code, advanced statistics, polished visualization, mastery of every tool, and a correct answer from the beginning are not required.
