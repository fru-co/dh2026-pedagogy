# DH2026 Week 3 Handout

## Title

William Austin, “Peter Rugg, the Missing Man”

## 1. Today's Theme

This week we read the text through the lens of repetition. In particular, we ask how recurring images of movement, storm, disappearance, and temporal dislocation create the text’s uncanny and unstable atmosphere.

## 2. What Matters This Week

### 1. Look beyond single words

This week, do not focus only on isolated words. Also look for repeated expressions and clusters of related language.

### 2. Treat repetition as structure

Repeated words or scenes may not be accidental. They may be tied to the structure and effect of the narrative.

### 3. Connect results to narrative effect

Do not stop with counting. Ask what the repetition makes the reader feel or notice.

## 3. Today's Questions

1. Through what repeated words or expressions are movement, storm, disappearance, and temporal dislocation created?
2. What uncanny effect is produced by the recurrence of certain words or phrases?
3. How can we connect frequency, paragraph-level clustering, and repeated expression to interpretation?

## 4. What We Will Do Today

1. Share comments
2. Choose one word or repeated expression
3. Review frequency and distribution
4. Inspect local contexts when useful
5. Consider where repetition clusters in the text
6. Summarize what kind of effect the repetition creates
