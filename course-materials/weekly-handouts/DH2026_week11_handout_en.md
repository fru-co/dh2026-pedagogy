# DH2026 Week 11 Handout

## Title

James McCune Smith, "The Washerwoman"

## 1. Today's Theme

This week we read James McCune Smith's "The Washerwoman" and spend more time using SBERT Text Lab, which we introduced last week. Because we had limited time to use the app in Week 10, we will use this shorter text to practice the difference between keyword search and semantic search.

## 2. What Matters This Week

### 1. A short text is useful for app practice

"The Washerwoman" is short, but it condenses major issues such as labor, the body, sound, religion, slavery, and freedom. Because the text is short, we can return quickly from the search results to the whole text.

### 2. Search results are entry points, not answers

SBERT Text Lab is a tool for finding semantically related passages. However, model results are not answers to literary interpretation. After seeing the result, we always reread the surrounding passage.

### 3. Generative AI is not the main tool this week

The syllabus originally placed generative AI analysis around Week 11, but as a continuation of last week, this week prioritizes hands-on practice with SBERT Text Lab. We will return to generative AI in the final paper and later methodological comparison.

## 3. Today's Questions

1. How does "The Washerwoman" represent labor as bodily exhaustion?
2. What effect does the repeated sound "Dunk! dunk!" have on the rhythm and meaning of the text?
3. How is the washerwoman's labor connected to freedom, slavery, religion, and family?
4. How do passages found through keyword search differ from passages found through SBERT semantic search?
5. Compared with Stowe's "The Minister's Housekeeper," how does women's domestic labor appear differently?
