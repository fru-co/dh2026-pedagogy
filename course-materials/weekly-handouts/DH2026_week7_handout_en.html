DH2026 Week 7 Handout

Title

Nathaniel Hawthorne, "Ethan Brand"

1. Today's Theme

This week we read Hawthorne's "Ethan Brand" and ask how images of sin, heart, fire, and coldness are connected. We continue working with word vectors and word embeddings, but this time students will use Word Vector Explorer themselves to experiment with word proximity.

2. What Matters This Week

1. Observe the semantic neighborhood of key words

Words such as sin, heart, fire, and marble do not carry meaning only by themselves. By observing what gathers around them, we can begin to see how the story organizes morality, bodily sensation, and emotion.

2. Bring vector results back to the text

The results from the Word Vector Explorer are not the answer to the story. A list of nearby words is a starting point for asking what cultural or sensory associations a word may carry. After looking at the results, always return to the literary text.

3. Value your own friction more than polished answers

In this exercise, you do not need to produce a perfect interpretation. It is more important to notice things like: "I do not understand why these words are close," "this result differs from my impression of the text," or "this gap is interesting."

3. Today's Questions

  1. What images are connected to sin, heart, fire, and marble in the story?
  2. Do the nearby words in the Word Vector Explorer match your impression of the text, or do they differ from it?
  3. Is heart closer to warmth, emotion, and life, or to coldness, hardness, and emptiness?
  4. Can fire be read as purification, destruction, passion, labor, or hell?
  5. After seeing the vector results, which part of the text do you want to reread?

4. What We Will Do Today

  1. Share comments
  2. Review the structure of the story and Hawthorne's problem of sin
  3. Learn how to use the Word Vector Explorer
  4. Choose one word and observe its nearby words
  5. Note where the results match or differ from your reading of the text
  6. Create a small map of the semantic neighborhood of one key word
