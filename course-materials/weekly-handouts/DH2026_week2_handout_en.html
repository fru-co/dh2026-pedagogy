DH2026 Week 2 Handout

Title

Charles Brockden Brown, “Somnambulism: A Fragment”

1. Today's Theme

This week we move from the introductory discussion into our first literary text and experiment with a KWIC (Keyword in Context) style of reading. The point is not to trust the machine’s output as a final answer, but to compare keyword patterns with your own close reading.

2. What Matters This Week

1. Form a hypothesis first

Before looking at the tool output, make a guess about what kind of anxiety, instability, or pattern a word might reveal.

2. Look at context, not only counts

Do not focus only on frequency. Look at the contexts in which the word appears.

3. Return from results to the text

After viewing the output, return to the text and ask how your interpretation has changed.

3. Today's Questions

  1. Through which words do sleepwalking, bodily sensation, and unstable perception appear?
  2. When we view one word across the text, do we notice repetitions or biases that ordinary reading might miss?
  3. What does KWIC or word distribution make visible, and what does it fail to show?

4. What We Will Do Today

  1. Share comments
  2. Choose one keyword
  3. Use the in-class KWIC tool to inspect its contexts
  4. Check the word’s distribution
  5. Connect the result back to specific passages
  6. Write a short experiment memo
