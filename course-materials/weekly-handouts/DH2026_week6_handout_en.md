# DH2026 Week 6 Handout

## Title

Catharine Maria Sedgwick, “Cacoethes Scribendi”

## 1. Today's Theme

This week we ask how writing itself becomes a subject of the story. By looking at words such as `write`, `author`, `letter`, `publish`, `read`, and `paper`, we consider how writing is represented as desire, action, and social position in the text.

## 2. What Matters This Week

### 1. Read writing as a theme

In this text, the important question is not only what is written, but who writes, why they write, and how writing is viewed.

### 2. Move from distribution to semantic neighborhood

So far, we have asked where words appear. This week we also ask what kinds of words appear near them, and what kinds of contexts they share.

### 3. Think of word vectors as similar contexts

Word vectors or word embeddings do not understand meaning as a dictionary definition. They treat words that appear in similar contexts as close to one another. In the Colab demonstration, we use `glove-wiki-gigaword-50`, a pre-trained model created with GloVe. The goal is not to learn the mathematics of GloVe, but to use the idea that words appearing in similar contexts become close as a clue for rereading the literary text.

## 3. Today's Questions

3. In this story, is writing represented closer to freedom, desire, comedy, or social recognition?
4. What can distribution and nearby words reveal that close reading alone might miss?
