## Title

Edgar Allan Poe, "The Man of the Crowd"

## 1. Today's Theme

This week we read Poe's "The Man of the Crowd" while introducing methods for vocabulary research with Word Vector Explorer and HistWords Explorer. Today we focus on how to choose words, what to compare them with, and how to bring search results back to close reading.

## 2. What Matters This Week

### 1. Vocabulary search is not just typing a word

Before choosing a search word, we need to decide what we want to compare. The meaning of a search changes depending on whether we compare the same word across time, compare similar words, or compare the same word across corpora.

### 2. Word Vector Explorer is a tool for observing word proximity

Word Vector Explorer does not give dictionary definitions. It uses word vectors to represent words that appear in similar contexts in large corpora as close to one another. The result is not an answer, but a tool for generating hypotheses that should lead us back to the text.

### 3. HistWords is a tool for observing historical semantic change

HistWords uses decade-level word embeddings to observe how the words near a target word change over time. If we examine how words such as `crowd`, `city`, `man`, `criminal`, or `observe` shift between the nineteenth and twentieth centuries, we can create historical questions for literary interpretation.
