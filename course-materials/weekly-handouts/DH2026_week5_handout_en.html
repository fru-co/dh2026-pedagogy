DH2026 Week 5 Handout

Title

Washington Irving, “The Devil and Tom Walker”

1. Today's Theme

This week we begin Layer 2. Until now, we have used frequency, KWIC, and distribution to see where words appear in a text. Today we move from individual words to relations between words, repeated phrases, and words that tend to appear together.

2. What Matters This Week

1. Move from words to phrases

We look not only at words such as devil or money, but also at the words that appear with them.

2. Read n-grams

An n-gram is a sequence of n words. A two-word sequence is a bigram, and a three-word sequence is a trigram. In literary study, n-grams can help us notice repeated phrasing and patterned combinations of words.

3. Read co-occurrence

Co-occurrence means that certain words tend to appear near one another. If words such as Tom, Walker, money, soul, or bargain cluster around devil, that pattern can become evidence for interpretation.

3. Today's Questions

  1. How are words such as devil, money, gold, bargain, and soul connected in “The Devil and Tom Walker”?
  2. How do repeated phrases or word combinations relate to the story’s moral lesson or satire?
  3. How does our reading change when we move from single words to n-grams and co-occurrence?

4. What We Will Do Today

  1. Share comments
  2. Review the basic structure of the story
  3. Introduce n-grams and co-occurrence
  4. Choose one target word and observe nearby words
  5. Bring repeated phrases and word combinations back into interpretation
  6. Write a short analysis note that could be useful for presentations
