DH2026 Week 1 Handout

Title

Introduction: How to Go Digital in Literary Studies

1. Today's Theme

In this course, we will read literary texts closely while experimenting with small-scale methods from Digital Humanities. The goal is not to master advanced technology from the start, but to move back and forth between human reading and digital analysis in order to form new questions of your own.

2. What Matters in This Course

1. Experiment over perfection

Even unsuccessful experiments count as meaningful work in this course.

2. Moving between close reading and digital analysis

We do not treat digital output as a final answer. We always return to the text.

3. Commenting and sharing

Comments in Google Docs are one of the central activities of this course.

3. Today's Questions

  1. Can a machine read a text?
  2. Where do human impressions and simple frequency counts overlap, and where do they diverge?
  3. What do digital methods add to literary study, and what might they diminish?

4. What We Will Do Today

  1. Review the goals of the course
  2. Write comments in Google Docs
  3. Try a simple frequency analysis on a short text
  4. Put both results and discomfort into words

5. Today's Activities

Activity 1: Write a Comment

  • Choose one sentence that stood out
  • Explain why it caught your attention
  • Note what you might want to examine digitally

Activity 2: Frequency Exercise

  • Check which words appear most often
  • Compare the results with your own reading impression

Activity 3: Reflection

  • What did you try?
  • What did you notice?
  • What still did not feel convincing?

6. After-Class Submission

Experiment Memo

  • What did you try?
  • What did you notice?
  • What did not work?
  • What do you want to try next?
