DH2026 Week 4 Handout

Title

James Kirke Paulding, “The End of the World: A Vision”

1. Today's Theme

This week we read the text through the lenses of apocalyptic vocabulary and satire. We ask how words such as world, fire, heat, prophet, progress, and money form clusters and how those clusters function not only as apocalypse, but also as social critique and satire.

2. What Matters This Week

1. Clarify the genre frame first

This text makes more sense when read not as straightforward realism, but as a combination of vision, apocalyptic fantasy, and satire.

2. Treat words as clusters

This week, do not isolate single words only. Notice how related words gather into clusters.

3. Connect lexical clusters to social critique

Do not focus only on end-of-the-world imagery. Also ask how that language connects to money, crime, progress, and prophecy.

3. Today's Questions

  1. Where do end-of-the-world words cluster, and in what kinds of scenes?
  2. What semantic groupings emerge around fire / heat / smoke / world / prophet / progress / money?
  3. How can this text be read not just as apocalypse, but as social satire?

4. What We Will Do Today

  1. Share comments
  2. Clarify the genre frame before analysis
  3. Choose one target word or cluster
  4. Review frequency, distribution, and nearby words
  5. Consider how the cluster connects to satire
  6. Write a short interpretation memo
